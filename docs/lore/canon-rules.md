# Canon Rules

These rules explain why the lore behaves the way it does. They are working canon and should be preserved unless a later revision deliberately replaces them.

## Ethereal Limits

- The Ethers have vast knowledge, but time has corroded even the smartest minds.
- The Ethers are ancient and powerful, not omniscient.
- The Ethers are only all-powerful within their known universe.
- The Ethers are the last species known to themselves, not necessarily the last species in all existence.
- All Ethers sealed their consciousnesses inside immortal avatar bodies.
- Ether avatar bodies are made from a material that negates all physical attacks.
- Ethereum has a hierarchy despite Ethereal immortality.
- The current ruling class is the Council of Morality, a working name for the seven mightiest Ethers.
- No one knows how much of the Ethers' original kindness remains after time corroded them. Only the Ethers themselves know the extent of that loss.
- Their biggest flaw is arrogance: they understand systems but underestimate mortality, scarcity, fear, reproduction, and desperation.
- They view Nogias as property and as a fun experiment, not as an equal civilization.

## Nogias Experiment

- The Ethers created Nogias to watch action, movement, and life after an eternity without meaningful conflict.
- Humans were given ambition and curiosity because those traits would make the experiment dynamic.
- Human ambition eventually escaped the Ethers' control.
- Nogias was chosen because it was far enough away that direct travel was difficult, but still close enough for Ethereal observation.

## Non-Intervention Doctrine

- Ethers are supposed to watch Nogias, not directly interfere with its affairs.
- Direct violence is considered beneath the Ethers.
- Indirect correction, containment, punishment, and engineered "natural consequence" are considered acceptable by Ethereal doctrine.
- Maria was punished because she broke the rule of non-intervention by helping the Elves directly.

## Magic Rules

- Magic began as the private weapon and final defense of the Ethers.
- Magic is a metaphysical force.
- Magic can harm Ethers because it bypasses or affects what their physical-attack-negating avatar bodies cannot protect.
- Maria taught Magic to the Elves out of compassion, not ambition.
- Maria acted because the Elves were submissive, defenseless, and viewed by her as true children of the Ethers.
- Once Magic entered Nogias, it seeped through life, land, language, memory, or other deep foundations of the world.
- Because Magic seeped into Nogias, the Ethers cannot cleanly remove it without destroying or maiming the world.
- Humans later gained access to Magic through Elven teaching, but only rarely. Very few Humans can actually conjure Magic.
- The Elves who taught Humans did so because some Humans sincerely sought wisdom and knowledge, and the Elves appreciated that pursuit.

## Elven Doctrine

- Elves are not weak.
- Elves follow a doctrine that forbids escalation because they remember, believe, or intuit that violence changes the world permanently.
- Elves represent the good part of the Ethers, the part corroded by time among the Gods themselves.

## Lidium Rules

- Maria was the first prisoner of Lidium and became Airam, "The Original Sin."
- Later rebellious or dissident Ethers were also cast into Lidium.
- Some of those imprisoned Ethers became archdemons.
- Archdemons may worship Airam, hate her, blame her, or fight over her legacy.
- After millennia of suffering, archdemons adapted and learned to create many kinds of demons to build armies.
- Lidium is the official name of the prison-world; Hell is the more common Human religious name for it.

## Human Religious Names

- Humans tend to interpret the realms through religion.
- Humans call the Ethers Gods.
- Humans call Ethereum Heaven.
- Humans call Lidium Hell.

## Portal Rules

- Alfredo was a rare Human magician who accidentally found a resonance that opened a gate to Ethereum, the realm Humans call Heaven.
- The Gods did not teach Humans how to reach Ethereum.
- The Gods seeded incomplete dimensional-travel knowledge that bends portals toward Lidium unless corrected by forbidden Magic.
- Portals have a soul-mass or authority limit.
- Lesser demons can pass through ordinary portal wounds.
- Archdemons must widen the wound through conquest, sacrifice, towers, rituals, corrupted land, or similar escalation before they can fully enter Nogias.
