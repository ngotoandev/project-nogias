# Races of Nogias

## Beastmen

Beastmen inherited the physical prowess of the Ethers. With inhuman strength, they became arrogant and aggressive.

In early Nogias history, Beastmen easily dominated other races through sheer physical superiority. They tended to stay in small groups, rely on their bodies rather than tools or large institutions, and scatter across the world.

As Humans grew stronger, Beastmen were killed off and forced into scattered survival. They remained a dominant force in direct physical terms, but no longer controlled the direction of world history.

## Elves

Elves inherited the wisdom of the Ethers. They became a peaceful race that preferred knowledge, craft, and harmony over conquest.

Elves could fight evenly against Beastmen by inventing tools such as weapons and traps, but their main priority was living in peace. They avoided fights whenever possible and lived in secluded forest groups.

The Ethers favored the Elves because the Elves reflected the good part of the Ethers: smart, generous, kind, and amiable. They represented the part of the Ethers that time had corroded.

When Humans rose, Elves were killed off and forced to flee into deep forests. They were not weak, but they followed a doctrine that forbade escalation because they remembered, believed, or intuited that violence changes the world permanently. Their population was decimated, and at times they were thought extinct.

Maria later taught Magic to the Elves out of compassion, allowing them to defend themselves and thrive again in the deep forests.

## Humans

Humans inherited the ambition and curiosity of the Ethers. They did not receive the physical strength of Beastmen or the wisdom of Elves, making them the weakest race at first.

Human weakness forced adaptation. Humans grouped together into towns to defend against Beastmen. They used Beastmen hide, claws, and fur to make armor and weapons. They sent emissaries to Elven kingdoms to learn new tools and traps.

Over time, Humans built layered defenses, reproduced exponentially, formed armies, and began to dominate Nogias.

Humanity's core danger is not raw power but hunger: greed, curiosity, expansion, and the refusal to remain limited.

After Maria taught Magic to the Elves, Humans eventually sought the Elves out and convinced some of them to teach Magic. Humans are diverse: some are destructive, but many sincerely seek wisdom. The Elves who trusted those Humans taught them Magic out of appreciation for knowledge. Magic became available to Humans only in rare cases. Very few Humans could actually conjure it, but the few who could became historically important.

## Power Shift

The first balance of Nogias was based on inherited Ethereal traits. Beastmen had strength, Elves had wisdom, and Humans had drive.

The power of the three races shifted because Humans adapted while the other two races did not. Beastmen relied on what they already were. Elves avoided escalation until survival demanded it. Humans copied, harvested, organized, reproduced, and improved.

That shift is one of the earliest tragedies of Nogias: the race that began with the least became the force that broke the balance of the world.
