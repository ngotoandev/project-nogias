# Worldbuilding Lore Bible

This folder tracks raw worldbuilding notes for Project Nogias. These notes are not written as novel prose yet; they are a structured reference for game design, faction writing, quests, unit identity, and future narrative drafts.

## Current Premise

Nogias is a created world. The beings later called Gods by Humans began as the Ethers, an ancient race that conquered their known universe, sealed all of their consciousnesses inside immortal avatar bodies, and eventually outlived every other species known to them.

After an eternity without war, conflict, or meaningful change, even the smartest Ethereal minds became corroded by time. The Ethers created Nogias as a hidden living world and a fun experiment they could watch from afar. They made three mortal races and gave each race one Ethereal trait:

- Beastmen inherited physical prowess.
- Elves inherited wisdom.
- Humans inherited ambition and curiosity.

The experiment did not remain balanced. Humans adapted faster than the other races, built towns and armies, learned from Elves, harvested Beastmen for materials, and eventually became the dominant force on Nogias. Human ambition was meant to create action and movement, but it grew beyond Ethereal control.

The Ethers favored the Elves because the Elves resembled the good part of the Ethers: wise, generous, kind, and amiable. No one knows how much of that kindness still remains in the Ethers after time corroded them. When the Elves were nearly destroyed, Maria broke Ethereal law and taught them Magic out of compassion. Magic is a metaphysical force and had been the Ethers' private weapon and final defense against their creations. Once Magic entered Nogias, it seeped through the world and could not be cleanly removed. With Magic, mortal races gained the theoretical power to kill their creators.

Maria was tried and condemned for treason. The Ethers built Lidium, a parallel prison-world for sin, and Maria became Airam, "The Original Sin." Later dissident Ethers who opposed Maria's punishment or formed forbidden attachments to Nogias were also cast into Lidium. Some became archdemons. Lidium is the official name of the realm Humans commonly call Hell.

Later history includes humanity's rare adoption of Elven Magic, Alfredo's accidental gate to Ethereum, the first intrusion into the realm Humans call Heaven, and the realm war between Nogias and the prison-world.

## Naming Rules

- Names that began in brackets, such as `<Nogias>` or `<Ethers>`, are treated as working names for now.
- Keep current names unless a later naming pass deliberately changes them.
- Glossary headings do not need brackets, but entries should note when a name is still provisional.
- Prefer preserving the idea first and polishing the wording later.
