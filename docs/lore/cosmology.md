# Cosmology

## Ethers and Gods

The Ethers were an ancient race that conquered their known universe a very long time ago. They managed to seal all of their consciousnesses inside immortal avatar bodies, allowing them to outlive all other species known to them.

After a very long time, the Ethers became the only species left within their own knowledge. There were no wars, no conflicts, and no meaningful external threats. Life continued that way for an eternity.

Over that eternity, the Ethers amassed an unfathomable amount of knowledge. Time also corroded even their smartest minds. They became ancient and powerful, but not omniscient. They are only all-powerful within their known universe, and their long peace made them arrogant toward mortal life.

No one knows how much of their original kindness remains. Only the Ethers themselves know the extent of what time has corroded.

Humans later know them as Gods because of humanity's religious tendency to interpret powerful unseen creators as divine beings.

## Ethereal Hierarchy

All Ethers became immortal avatar bodies, but Ethereum is not equal in power or authority.

An Ether's consciousness is sealed inside its avatar body. These bodies are made from a material that negates all physical attacks, which is why ordinary weapons cannot harm Ethers.

The current ruling class is the Council of Morality, a working name for the seven mightiest Ethers. The final name for this council still needs review.

## The Creation of Nogias

The Ethers became bored with endless peace and immortality. They wanted action and movement, so they created Nogias as a fun experiment. They chose a planet that was not too far from their reach, but far enough that direct travel would be difficult and their presence could remain hidden.

Nogias was not a naturally independent world from the Ethers' perspective. It was a constructed world and, at least at first, an experiment or entertainment watched from a distance.

The Ethers considered Nogias property, not an equal civilization.

## Mortal Design

The Ethers created three races and gave each one a selected Ethereal characteristic:

- Beastmen received the Ethers' physical prowess.
- Elves received the Ethers' wisdom.
- Humans received the Ethers' ambition and curiosity.

The races developed around those traits in ways the Ethers did not fully control. The Beastmen leaned into strength, the Elves leaned into peace and knowledge, and Humans compensated for weakness through adaptation, expansion, and hunger for more.

Humans were given ambition and curiosity because the Ethers wanted the world to move. That same ambition eventually escaped their control.

## Non-Intervention Doctrine

Ethers are supposed to watch Nogias, not directly take part in its affairs. Direct intervention is forbidden.

The Ethers consider direct violence beneath them, but they accept indirect correction, containment, punishment, and engineered "natural consequence." This doctrine lets them see themselves as divine and restrained even when they cause enormous suffering.

## Magic

Magic is a metaphysical force. It was originally the sole proprietary power of the Ethers, their weapon and last defense against others.

The Ethers kept Magic from Nogias because it was the one force that could let mortal races threaten their makers. Physical attacks are negated by Ether avatar bodies, but Magic can bypass or affect what those bodies cannot protect. If the creatures of Nogias ever rose against the Ethers, the Ethers expected to use Magic to suppress them easily.

Maria broke that rule by teaching Magic to the Elves out of pure compassion. She saw the Elves as submissive, defenseless, and close to true children of the Ethers.

Once Magic entered Nogias, it seeped through the world. It could not be cleanly removed from life, land, language, memory, or whatever deeper foundation it had touched. With Magic, creatures of Nogias inherently gained the power to kill even their creators.

## Lidium

After Maria taught Magic to the Elves, the Ethers condemned, tried, and convicted her of treason.

They created a prison just for Maria and called it Lidium, a prison for sins. Lidium is a parallel world, just like Nogias.

Maria became Airam, "The Original Sin." Other Ethers also felt a strong connection to the Elves and to Nogias, and some were not happy with Maria's punishment. Dissidents were thrown into Lidium after her.

Lidium became a prison-world for sins. Lidium is its official name, while Hell is the more common Human religious name for it. Later rebellious Ethers imprisoned there became archdemons. Some worship Airam, some hate her, and some blame her.

After millennia of suffering, archdemons adapted to Lidium and learned to create many kinds of demons to build their armies.

## Realms

The current realm structure is:

- Ethereum, Heaven, or the Ethereal realm: the hidden realm of the Ethers. Ethereum is the official name, while Heaven is the more common Human religious name.
- Nogias: the created mortal world of Beastmen, Elves, Humans, and other later races.
- Lidium: the prison-world for sins, created first for Maria/Airam. Lidium is the official name, while Hell is the more common Human religious name.

The later realm war begins when dimensional travel exposes the boundaries between these worlds.

Dimensional travel obeys limits. Ordinary portal wounds cannot carry beings with too much soul-mass or authority, so lesser demons can pass before archdemons can. Archdemons must widen the wound through conquest, sacrifice, towers, rituals, corrupted land, or similar escalation.
